00 · Restricted Fireworks

Documentation is required before restricted orders move forward.

American Tradition LLC offers both 1.4g articles pyrotechnic grade fireworks and 1.3g display grade fireworks. These products require special documentation before we can order, hold, ship, or release them for pickup.

When a customer opens a professional or display product, the site shows a restricted-fireworks disclaimer first. The full manual below explains what paperwork is needed, how shipping works, and what to do if freight arrives damaged.

Quick Start

Before we start.

  • Send your documentation before we process the order.
  • Contact us if you need help getting the right training or license.
  • Freight claims must be documented in writing within 48 hours.

02 · Process

What we need before order, hold, ship, or pickup.

  1. Send documentation for review

    Before we can get started with a restricted-fireworks order, you need to send the required documentation for review. If you want to order restricted products and do not yet have the proper paperwork, contact us and we can help verify what you need.

    In many cases, the required documents are straightforward to obtain. If you have questions about the basics, contact a sales representative for more information.

  2. Include this with your message

    • Your full name and company name, if applicable
    • The product category or SKU you want to order
    • Your destination state and whether the order will ship or be picked up
    • Your PGI or training certificate, or Federal Explosives License (FEL)
03 · Shipping

Freight terms, limits, and customer responsibilities.

Where We Ship

American Tradition LLC cannot ship outside the continental United States. We do not ship to Alaska, Hawaii, Canada, U.S. territories, or any other countries.

Shipping rates are subject to change and are calculated during checkout before payment. Orders ship FOB origin, which means ownership transfers to the customer once the order is loaded on the freight carrier's truck.

Region
Continental U.S. only
Excluded
AK, HI, Canada, U.S. territories
Terms
FOB origin · Title transfers at dock

Delivery Services & Disputes

Extra freight services provided at delivery are the sole responsibility of the customer and are not included in paid or free shipping.

  • We do not include extra freight services in our shipping.
  • If you receive a bill, we can help explain the charge.
  • You must file disputes directly with the freight carrier.
  • We cannot file a carrier dispute on your behalf.
04 · Freight Claims

Inspect deliveries immediately. Document problems fast.

48-Hour Deadline

At Delivery

Inspect your shipment as soon as it arrives. Note any visible damage or missing items on the driver's bill of lading and have the driver sign for the discrepancy before leaving.

All freight claims must be submitted in writing by email within 48 hours of delivery to the freight company. Claims received after 48 hours may not be eligible because carriers enforce strict deadlines.

Helpful Information for Claims

  • Photos of outer and inner damage
  • Order number
  • First and last name
  • List of damaged or missing items
