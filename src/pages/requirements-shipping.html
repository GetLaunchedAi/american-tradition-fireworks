00 · Restricted Fireworks

Documentation comes first. The order moves second.

We sell two regulated classes: 1.4g articles pyrotechnic (consumer-grade) and 1.3g display ordnance (professional-grade). Both require a file on hand before we'll order, hold, ship, or release for pickup.

Open a professional or display product on the shop and you'll see a restricted-fireworks notice up front. The manual below spells out the paperwork, the freight terms, and what to do when a pallet shows up beat.

01 · What We Need Schedule A

1.4g articles and 1.3g display ordnance carry different paperwork. What you ship, store, and shoot decides which file we need on hand.

Product Class Documentation Storage Rule
1.4G · Articles Consumer-grade pyrotechnic PGI certificate, certified training-course certificate, or FEL No federal storage required.
1.3G · Display Professional display ordnance Federal Explosives License (FEL) — no substitutes. Federal magazine storage required if held more than one day.
02 · Process

Submit, verify, order, ship.

  1. Send documentation for review.

    Email the file before we open the order. New to this and don't yet hold the right paperwork? Call — we'll tell you which class fits and which credential is required.

    Already hold an FEL? Apply for wholesale →

  2. Include this with your message.

    • Full name and company name (if applicable).
    • Product category or SKU.
    • Destination state. Ship or pickup.
    • PGI card, training certificate, or Federal Explosives License (FEL).

  3. We verify the file.

    Documents go through review before the order touches inventory. Anything missing or expired, we call back the same day.

  4. Order ships or holds for pickup.

    Once cleared, the order moves. Freight ships FOB origin. Pickup orders stage at the originating store and release on a signed bill of lading.

03 · Shipping Terms of Sale

Freight terms, limits, and customer responsibilities.

Where we ship.

Continental United States only. We don't ship to Alaska, Hawaii, Canada, U.S. territories, or anywhere else outside the lower 48. No exceptions.

Rates change. Final freight gets calculated at checkout before you pay. Orders move FOB origin (Free On Board) — title transfers the moment the pallet is loaded on the carrier's truck.

Continental U.S. only

Delivery services & disputes.

Extra services at delivery — lift gate, inside delivery, residential surcharges, redelivery — ride on the customer. They are not bundled into paid or free shipping.

  • Extra freight services are not included in our shipping.
  • Get a separate bill from the carrier? We'll help read it.
  • Disputes go to the freight carrier directly.
  • We don't file carrier disputes on your behalf.
04 · Freight Claims

Inspect at delivery. Document fast.

48-Hour Deadline

The freight-claim window is 48 hours from delivery. Miss it and the carrier will deny the file. We will not be able to override the deadline on your behalf.

At delivery.

Inspect the shipment as it comes off the truck. Visible damage or short count goes on the driver's bill of lading in writing — have the driver sign for the discrepancy before they leave the dock.

Submit the claim in writing, by email, to the freight carrier within 48 hours of delivery. Carriers enforce the window strictly. Late files get denied.

Information for the claim file.

  • Photos of outer and inner damage.
  • Order number.
  • First and last name.
  • List of damaged or missing items.
